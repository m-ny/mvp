# Week 13 Final Presentation - Slides 8-13 Script

Bullet-form speaker script for the final pitch.
Designed to match the Week 12 executive storytelling rules:
- short sentences
- clear transitions
- one proof point per slide
- demo clearly marked

---

## Slide 8 - The Reality vs. The Impact

- "Today, the reality is manual outreach fatigue." [pause]
- "CAs spend time guessing who to contact and what to say."
- "So volume stays low, and follow-up quality is inconsistent."
- "Our impact is not just efficiency." [pause]
- "It is more qualified outreach at scale."
- "By matching client memory to timely trends, we help CAs turn more messages into meaningful conversations and, ultimately, more boutique appointments."

---

## Slide 9 - The Inputs

- "Before the agent writes anything, it needs the right inputs." [pause]
- "We use three sources."
- "First, client memory: preferences, past conversations, and life events."
- "Second, trend data: what is relevant this week and why it matters."
- "Third, the product catalog: real products, with real attributes."
- "So the system is not inventing context from scratch." [pause]
- "It is grounding outreach in memory, market signal, and product reality."

- "The other is for He Yawen, a mother looking for a classic coming-of-age gift for her daughter."

## Slide 12 - Test Design

- "We tested this in two layers." [pause]
- "First, the automated evaluation flow."
- "The outreach agent generated one result package for each benchmark client."
- "That package included the angle, the drafted messages, the evidence, and the next step."
- "Then an independent evaluation agent reviewed each package."
- "It scored three things: groundedness, over-promotion, and would a CA send it."
- "Each dimension was scored from one to five."
- "Then we aggregated the scores into one report and used the weakest cases to refine the prompt."
- "Our sample was 20 benchmark client cases, evaluated in one batch on March 30."
- "Then we added a human layer." [pause]
- "We also collected real reviewer feedback on whether these drafts felt useful, trustworthy, and brand-appropriate."
- "The clearest human takeaway was this:"
- "The strong drafts felt CRM-aware and practical."
- "The weak drafts were usually too long, too product-led, or needed a final human edit."

---

## Slide 13 - Next Steps

- "So what do we do next?" [pause]
- "The next step is not another demo improvement." [pause]
- "It is the path from MVP to a real LVMH product."
- "First, connect product information to real official data."
- "Right now, product knowledge is simulated."
- "In a real product, it should come from live catalog or website data, so recommendations reflect real assortments."
- "Second, connect the system to CRM."
- "That gives the agent richer and more persistent client memory: preferences, contact history, and prior interactions."
- "Third, add the product workflow around the model."
- "That means CA review, edit, and approval before anything is sent."
- "So the goal is not just better generation." [pause]
- "It is a trusted clienteling copilot built on real data and real workflow."
